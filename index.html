Pavan Vadgama Berlin and New York City-based architectural designer with 6+ years of professional experience in adaptive reuse, urban regeneration, and participatory design, and 2+ years of teaching at the graduate and undergraduate levels. Contributed to public discourse through exhibitions, talks, and writings on materiality and built heritage. As a graduate of Princeton's Post-Professional Master of Architecture program, received the Suzanne Kolarik Underwood Prize and the History and Theory Prize for research on advanced digital techniques in the activation of public space. Awarded the Howard Crosby Butler Fellowship for field research on stepwells in Ahmedabad, India. Earned a Master of Arts in Architecture from Berlin University of the Arts, with studies under Jean-Philippe Vassal, and a Bachelor of Arts in Architecture from UC Berkeley. Fluent in English and German, bringing a global perspective and a strong commitment to sustainable design.
